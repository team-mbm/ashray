ASHRAY - Inhibatation Center

Welcome to the Ashray Inhibitation Center (AIC)! We are a Non Government organisation (NGO) in various states of India, dedicated to give shelter to children in need. We work with 3000 or so people at different levels and collaborate with few other NGOs. AIC welcomes complaints from across the country, analyses the complaint and gives it a priority level. Children with higher priority get shelter at Ashray for minimum of 90 days.

The first focus of AIC is the health of the residents who face problems like overcrowding, malnutrition, and diseases like tuberculosis. To treat everyday ailments, AIC runs a free walk-in clinic open six days a week, and once a week, Dr. Bhatnagar comes in for free consultations, generously donating his time. To help prevent illness from occurring we also give our school children nutritious chyawanprash and milk each day and soap each month (pictured below).

Beyond meeting immediate needs, AIC is working for a brighter future. In our K-5 school, we teach approximately 70 children, not only about the core academic subjects, but about the essential topics of cleanliness, health, civic duty, and the environment. ACT’s evening coaching classes, serving students in classes 6-12, offer help with both schoolwork and state examination preparation. For women of all ages, ACT’s sewing classes teach a skill that is useful both for housework and earning wages.
